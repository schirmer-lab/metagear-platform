**MetaGEAR Explorer** is a web application and REST API for interrogating curated catalogs of microbial genes. BLAST a sequence, browse by Pfam domain, or explore Metagenomic Species Pangenomes — and see where matches show up across published microbiome cohorts in seconds.

It's a free hosted service. No software to install, no local setup.

→ **[Open MetaGEAR Explorer ↗](https://metagear-explorer.schirmerlab.de)**

## What's indexed

- **33M+** gene families across all cohorts
- **24** uniformly-processed metagenomic cohorts: IBD, colorectal cancer, healthy controls
- **9,053** stool samples
- **13,795** species pangenomes with gene catalogs

## What you can do with it

**Search by sequence.** Paste a nucleotide or protein sequence and run BLAST against the cohort-wide gene catalogs. Get hits ranked by identity and coverage, with the cohort, sample, and contig of origin attached to each.

**Search by domain.** Look up a Pfam domain (e.g. `PF08020`) and see every gene in the curated catalog that contains it, grouped by cohort and annotation.

**Explore MSPs.** Browse curated Metagenomic Species Pangenomes — see all the genes in a species pangenome, with cohort and sample provenance attached.

**Trace back to samples.** Every result links back to the cohort and sample metadata so you can judge biological relevance, not just sequence similarity.
